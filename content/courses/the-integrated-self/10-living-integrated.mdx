Integration isn't just a practice you do—it becomes a way of living. This final module explores how to bring everything you've learned into every area of your life, making wholeness not an occasional experience but an ongoing reality.

## Integration at Work

### Bringing Wholeness to Work

Integration at work means:
- Showing up authentically
- Managing parts that get triggered
- Using emotional intelligence
- Not abandoning yourself for job
- Integrating rather than fragmenting

### Common Work Challenges

Work can fragment through:
- Having to perform
- Dealing with difficult people
- Stress and overwhelm
- Power dynamics
- Work-life imbalance

### Integrated Work Presence

To stay integrated:
- Know what parts get activated at work
- Maintain body awareness
- Process emotions rather than suppress
- Set appropriate boundaries
- Stay connected to purpose

## Integration in Crisis

### When Life Falls Apart

Crisis can fragment or integrate:
- Trauma can split
- But crisis also cracks open
- Suffering can deepen
- Challenge can integrate

### Staying Integrated in Difficulty

In crisis:
- Double down on practice
- Lean on support
- Let parts be seen
- Process rather than suppress
- Find meaning in suffering

### Post-Crisis Integration

After crisis:
- Integrate what happened
- Learn from the experience
- Allow growth
- Update your parts map
- Deepen practice

## Signs of Integration

### How to Know It's Working

Integration shows up as:
- Less internal conflict
- More authentic presence
- Better emotional range
- Access to body wisdom
- Shadow less controlling
- Relationships improving
- Sense of meaning
- Life from center, not edges

### Not Perfection

Integration doesn't mean:
- No more struggle
- No more parts activation
- Perfect self-awareness
- Never getting triggered
- Having it all together

It means:
- Less suffering from fragmentation
- Quicker return to wholeness
- More Self-leadership
- Growing capacity
- Progress, not perfection

---

## Sustaining Integration

### Daily Practice

Non-negotiable:
- Some form of daily practice
- Morning and/or evening
- Brief pauses throughout day
- Regular body and parts check-in

### Weekly and Longer

Also important:
- Weekly deeper practice
- Periodic retreat or intensive
- Ongoing learning
- Community or support

### When You Fall Off

When practice lapses:
- Notice without judgment
- Begin again simply
- Don't wait to feel ready
- Return to basics
- Restart today
