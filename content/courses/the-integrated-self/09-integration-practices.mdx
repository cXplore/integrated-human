## Practice Makes Integration

Understanding integration isn't enough. Integration happens through practice—regular, ongoing engagement with the work of wholeness. This module provides practices you can use daily, weekly, and as needed to support ongoing integration.

---

## The Foundation: Daily Practice

### Why Daily Matters

Daily practice:
- Creates consistency
- Builds integration gradually
- Provides regular check-in
- Prevents fragmentation from accumulating
- Makes integration a way of life

### Core Daily Elements

A basic daily practice includes:
- **Morning:** Intention and check-in
- **Throughout day:** Awareness moments
- **Evening:** Reflection and release

### Starting Simple

Begin with:
- 10-15 minutes morning
- Brief pauses during day
- 5-10 minutes evening

Expand as capacity grows.

---

## Morning Integration Practice

### The Practice

A morning integration practice:

1. **Center** (2-3 minutes)
   - Sit quietly
   - Take deep breaths
   - Arrive in your body

2. **Check-in** (2-3 minutes)
   - How is my body?
   - What am I feeling?
   - What parts are present?
   - What needs attention?

3. **Intention** (2-3 minutes)
   - What does today ask of me?
   - How will I stay connected to Self?
   - What integration work is relevant today?

4. **Connection** (2-3 minutes)
   - Connect to something larger
   - Remember your purpose
   - Ground in meaning

## Parts Check-In Practice

### Working With Parts Daily

Regular parts practice:
1. Notice what parts are active
2. Check in with protectors
3. Sense for exiles that need attention
4. Connect with Self
5. Appreciate all parts

### The Check-In Questions

Ask yourself:
- What parts am I aware of right now?
- Is a protector active? What's it protecting?
- Is an exile present? What does it need?
- Can I access Self?
- What does the system need today?

### When to Use

Use parts check-in:
- Morning practice
- When triggered
- Before difficult situations
- After challenging interactions
- When feeling fragmented

## Body Integration Practice

### Daily Body Check-In

A body practice for integration:

1. **Scan** (1-2 minutes)
   - Move awareness through body
   - Notice sensations
   - Note areas of tension or ease

2. **Breathe** (1-2 minutes)
   - Breathe into tight areas
   - Soften with exhale
   - Let body relax

3. **Move** (2-5 minutes)
   - Gentle movement
   - Stretching
   - Whatever body wants

4. **Ground** (1 minute)
   - Feel feet on ground
   - Weight of body
   - Presence in physical form

## Emotional Processing Practice

### When Emotions Arise

When you notice emotion:

1. **Pause**
   - Stop what you're doing
   - Create space

2. **Notice**
   - What emotion is present?
   - Name it specifically

3. **Allow**
   - Let it be here
   - Don't try to change it

4. **Feel in body**
   - Where is it located?
   - What sensations?

5. **Listen**
   - What is it saying?
   - What does it need?

6. **Let it move**
   - Trust it will pass
   - Let the energy complete

## Shadow Awareness Practice

### Catching Shadow

Daily shadow awareness:
- Notice strong reactions to others
- Notice judgments you make
- Notice impulses you suppress
- Ask: "Is this my shadow?"

### Shadow Questions

When shadow appears, ask:
- What quality am I reacting to?
- Could I have this in me?
- What am I projecting?
- What would owning this give me?

### Brief Shadow Work

Quick shadow practice:
1. Notice the trigger
2. Name the quality you're reacting to
3. Find even a small piece in yourself
4. Say: "I have this too"
5. Feel what shifts

## Evening Integration Practice

### The Practice

An evening integration practice:

1. **Review** (2-3 minutes)
   - What happened today?
   - What was challenging?
   - What parts were active?
   - What did I learn?

2. **Integrate** (2-3 minutes)
   - What needs integration?
   - What emotion wasn't fully felt?
   - What part needs acknowledgment?
   - What can I let go of?

3. **Gratitude** (1-2 minutes)
   - What am I grateful for?
   - What went well?
   - What supported me?

4. **Release** (1-2 minutes)
   - Release the day
   - Prepare for rest
   - Set intention for sleep

## Weekly Integration Review

### Weekly Practice

Once per week:
- Longer review of the week
- Deeper integration work
- Planning for coming week
- Extended practice time

### Weekly Review Questions

Reflect on:
- What patterns emerged this week?
- What triggered me?
- What parts were active?
- What needs more attention?
- How was my integration work?
- What do I want to focus on next week?

---

## Integration in Difficult Moments

### When Fragmentation Happens

In crisis or difficulty:

1. **Pause** – Stop; breathe
2. **Ground** – Feel body, ground
3. **Parts check** – Who's active? Is this a part?
4. **Self access** – Find the calm center
5. **Respond** – Choose from Self, not reactive part

### Quick Integration Tools

For fast integration:
- Three conscious breaths
- Feel your feet
- Name what you're feeling
- Remind yourself: "This is a part, not all of me"
- Ask: "What would Self do here?"

## Complete Daily Practice

### Bringing It Together

A complete daily practice might include:

- Parts check-in
- Body scan
- Intention setting
- Meaning connection

**Throughout Day:**
- Brief pauses
- Emotional processing as needed
- Shadow awareness
- Grounding moments

- Day review
- Integration of unprocessed material
- Gratitude
- Release

## Key Concepts

- **Daily foundation:** Morning, throughout-day, and evening practices
- **Parts check-in:** Regularly noticing and relating to parts
- **Body practice:** Scanning, breathing, moving, grounding
- **Emotional processing:** Pause, notice, allow, feel, listen, let move
- **Shadow awareness:** Catching projections, owning what you find
- **Difficult moments:** Pause, ground, parts check, Self access, respond
- **Weekly review:** Deeper reflection and planning

---

<Callout type="important">
These practices are suggestions—adapt them to your life. What matters is consistent practice, not perfect practice. Start with what you can sustain, and let your practice evolve. The goal is ongoing integration, not one-time achievement.
</Callout>
