## Raising Your Threshold

### The Upper Limit Issue

Remember:
- You have a threshold for how much good you can tolerate
- Sabotage often happens when you exceed it
- Sustainable change requires raising this threshold

### How to Raise It

Gradually expand your tolerance:
- Notice when you're at the edge
- Stay there a little longer
- Breathe through the discomfort
- Prove you can handle more

### Practice

When things are going well:
- Notice the discomfort
- Don't sabotage
- Let yourself have the good
- Expand your capacity

## Updating Your Identity

### The Old Identity

Your old identity may include:
- "I'm someone who sabotages"
- "I can't trust myself"
- "I always mess things up"
- "Success isn't for me"

### The New Identity

Sustainable change requires a new identity:
- "I'm someone who follows through"
- "I'm learning to trust myself"
- "I handle success well"
- "I'm capable of lasting change"

### How Identity Shifts

Identity changes through:
- Repeated new actions
- Evidence that contradicts old beliefs
- New stories you tell yourself
- Time and consistency

## Environmental Design

### Your Environment Matters

Set yourself up for success:
- Remove triggers for sabotage
- Add cues for positive action
- Design your space to support goals
- Make good choices easier

### Examples

Environmental changes:
- Remove substances if self-medicating
- Put workout clothes out the night before
- Block distracting websites
- Keep healthy food visible and accessible

### The Power of Environment

Environment shapes behavior:
- Willpower is limited
- Environment is constant
- Design for success
- Don't rely on discipline alone

## Handling the Long Haul

### This Is a Practice

Sustainable change means:
- Ongoing attention, not one-time fix
- Practice, not perfection
- Progress over time
- Lifelong journey

### What to Expect

Along the way:
- Old patterns will resurface (this is normal)
- Progress won't be linear
- Some seasons will be harder
- You'll keep learning

### Returning

When you notice old patterns:
- Don't despair
- Use what you've learned
- Return to the practice
- Each return strengthens the new way

## Key Concepts

- **System:** Daily, weekly, and monthly practices for maintenance
- **Threshold:** Gradually raise your tolerance for good things
- **Identity:** Shift from "saboteur" to "someone who follows through"
- **Environment:** Design your surroundings to support success
- **Support:** Build relationships and accountability that help
- **Long haul:** This is practice, not perfection; a journey, not a destination
- **Commitment:** Declare what you're committed to and why

---

<Callout type="important">
Sustainable change isn't about never sabotaging again. It's about building a life where you mostly don't, and when you do, you recover quickly. It's about having systems that support you, an identity that aligns with your goals, and the self-trust to keep going. This is possible for you. Not through force or self-hatred, but through understanding, practice, and persistent compassion. The life on the other side is worth it.
