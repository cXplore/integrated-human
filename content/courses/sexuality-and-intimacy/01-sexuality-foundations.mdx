## Your Sexual Self

Sexuality is one of the most powerful forces in human experience—yet for many, it's also one of the most complicated.

Shame, confusion, performance anxiety, disconnection. Most of us carry baggage around sex that we rarely examine.

**Before you can transform your sexuality, you need to understand your current relationship with it.**

---

## What Is Sexuality?

### Beyond the Physical:
Sexuality is more than the mechanics of sex. It includes:
- Your relationship with your body
- Your desires and fantasies
- Your capacity for pleasure
- How you connect erotically with others
- Your sense of yourself as a sexual being

### A Core Part of Being Human:
Sexuality isn't separate from who you are. It's woven into your body, psyche, relationships, and spirit.

---

## How Sexual Identity Forms

### Influences on Your Sexuality:

**Early Messages:**
- What you were taught about sex (or what wasn't said)
- Religious or cultural attitudes
- How bodies were treated in your home
- Early experiences with shame or acceptance

**Formative Experiences:**
- First sexual experiences
- Positive or negative encounters
- Trauma (if applicable)
- Early relationships

**Ongoing Shaping:**
- Pornography and media
- Partner responses
- Your own discoveries
- Cultural messages

## Common Sexual Conditioning

### Messages Many Receive:

**About Sex Itself:**
- Sex is dirty/shameful
- Sex is only for procreation
- Sex is dangerous
- Nice people don't...
- Your desires are wrong

**About Gender:**
- Men should always want sex
- Women shouldn't want sex too much
- Certain expressions are forbidden
- Your body should look a certain way

**About Performance:**
- You must perform well
- Orgasm is the goal
- Your partner's pleasure is your job
- Sex should look like porn

## Your Current Relationship with Sexuality

### Key Questions:
- How do you feel about yourself as a sexual being?
- Are you comfortable with your desires?
- Can you be present during sex?
- Is there shame?
- What's working? What isn't?

### Different Relationships with Sexuality:
- **Avoidant:** Shutting down, avoiding
- **Compulsive:** Using sex to numb or avoid
- **Disconnected:** Going through motions
- **Anxious:** Worried about performance
- **Integrated:** Present, connected, alive

## Sexuality and the Body

### Embodiment Matters:
Your relationship with sexuality is deeply connected to your relationship with your body.

### Common Challenges:
- Disconnection from body
- Body shame
- Difficulty feeling pleasure
- Living "from the neck up"
- Tension and armor

### The Invitation:
Healing sexuality often involves healing your relationship with your body—learning to inhabit it, feel it, trust it.

---

## What Healthy Sexuality Looks Like

### Characteristics:
- **Present:** Fully in the experience, not in your head
- **Connected:** To yourself and partner
- **Pleasure-Oriented:** Enjoying the experience, not just the outcome
- **Shame-Free:** No toxic shame about desires or body
- **Communicative:** Can express needs and boundaries
- **Flexible:** Adapts to different contexts and ages
- **Integrated:** Part of whole self, not compartmentalized

### Not:
- Performance-focused
- Shame-driven
- Disconnected
- Only about orgasm
- Exactly like porn

Not a specific performance level—but an integrated, present, shame-free relationship with your sexuality.

## A Note on Trauma

### If You Have Sexual Trauma:
This course is educational, not therapeutic. If you have sexual trauma that's unprocessed:
- This material may be triggering
- Consider working with a trauma-informed therapist
- Go slowly with this content
- Skip exercises that feel unsafe
- Seek support as needed

### Trauma Doesn't Disqualify You:
Healing is possible. This course can be part of that—but may not be sufficient on its own.
