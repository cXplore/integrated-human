## Why We Leave

If presence is natural and beneficial, why are we so rarely there?

Because leaving the moment serves purposes. Understanding these purposes is the first step to coming back.

**You're not bad at presence. You've been trained out of it.**

---

## The Four Main Obstacles

What habitually pulls us from the present:

### 1. Rumination (The Past)
Living in replay mode

### 2. Worry (The Future)
Living in projection mode

### 3. Compulsive Thinking
The narrative mind's constant commentary

### 4. Numbing
Avoiding the present through distraction

---

## Obstacle 1: Rumination

### What It Looks Like:
- Replaying conversations
- Analyzing what happened
- Regretting decisions
- Rehearsing what you should have said
- Carrying grudges and grievances

### Why We Do It:
- Trying to make sense of things
- Hoping to change the past (impossible)
- Processing that never completes
- Habit of reviewing for danger

### The Cost:
- Missing the present entirely
- Extending past pain into now
- No actual resolution
- Increased anxiety and depression

## Obstacle 2: Worry

### What It Looks Like:
- Anticipating problems
- Playing out worst-case scenarios
- Planning for every contingency
- Anxious about what might happen
- Rehearsing future conversations

### Why We Do It:
- Attempt to control the uncontrollable
- Feels productive (it isn't)
- Nervous system scanning for danger
- Illusion of preparation

### The Cost:
- Suffering from things that never happen
- Living future problems twice
- Missing present opportunities
- Chronic anxiety and stress

## Obstacle 3: Compulsive Thinking

### What It Looks Like:
- Running commentary on everything
- Judging, evaluating, analyzing
- Narrating your experience
- Problem-solving that doesn't stop
- Mental noise that never quiets

### Why We Do It:
- Mind's default mode
- Feels like "me"
- Attempt to understand and control
- Habit since childhood

### The Cost:
- Never experiencing life directly
- Everything filtered through thought
- Exhaustion from mental activity
- Missing the simplicity of being

## Obstacle 4: Numbing

### What It Looks Like:
- Reaching for phone automatically
- Scrolling without purpose
- Substances to take the edge off
- Busyness as avoidance
- Entertainment that fills every gap

### Why We Do It:
- Avoiding uncomfortable feelings
- Escaping boredom or emptiness
- Nervous system dysregulation
- Not knowing how to just be

### The Cost:
- Never processing what needs processing
- Addiction to stimulation
- Inability to be with yourself
- Missing your actual life

## The Root: Discomfort with Now

Beneath all obstacles is the same thing:

### The Present Moment Can Be Uncomfortable:
- Emotions we don't want to feel
- Sensations we want to avoid
- Reality we don't want to face
- The rawness of being alive

### So We Leave:
- To the safety of thought
- To the distraction of activity
- To anywhere but here

### The Paradox:
- Leaving creates more suffering
- Running from discomfort extends it
- The present moment is actually bearable
- It's the resistance that hurts

## The Stories We Tell

Obstacles are maintained by stories:

### "I need to figure this out"
- Justifies endless rumination
- Thinking rarely solves emotional issues
- Understanding isn't healing

### "I'm being responsible by worrying"
- Worry feels productive
- Actual preparation is different
- Most worry is just suffering

### "If I stop thinking, I'll miss something"
- Trust in thought as safety
- Fear of direct experience
- Identity wrapped in thinking

### "I need something to do"
- Discomfort with stillness
- Avoiding inner experience
- Always running

---

## Working with Obstacles

The approach isn't fighting obstacles—it's understanding them:

### 1. Notice
- "Oh, I'm ruminating"
- "There's the worry"
- Without judgment

### 2. Understand
- What triggered it?
- What am I avoiding?
- What does this pattern serve?

### 3. Choose
- Stay with it consciously or
- Return to the present
- This moment, this breath, this sensation

### 4. Repeat
- Thousands of times
- It's a practice, not a fix
- Patience with the process

## The Invitation in Obstacles

### Obstacles Are Pointers:
- They show you what you're avoiding
- They reveal your patterns
- They indicate where healing is needed
- They're not enemies—they're information

### Behind Every Obstacle:
- Something wanting to be felt
- Something needing attention
- Life trying to happen
- The present moment waiting

---

## Key Concepts

- **Four Main Obstacles:** Rumination (past), worry (future), compulsive thinking, numbing.

- **Root Cause:** Discomfort with the present moment.

- **Maintained by Stories:** Beliefs that justify leaving the present.

- **Working With:** Notice, understand, choose, repeat.
