## The Question We Avoid

You are going to die.

Everyone you love is going to die.

This is not pessimism. It's fact. And yet most of us live as though it weren't true.

**This course is about facing that truth—not to depress you, but to liberate you.**

---

## What Death Awareness Offers

Those who face death consciously report:
- Greater clarity about what matters
- Release of petty concerns and grudges
- More presence in daily life
- Deeper relationships
- Less fear overall
- More authentic choices
- Urgency for living fully

**Paradoxically, facing death is one of the most life-giving things you can do.**

---

## The Cost of Denial

Most people live in denial of death. This denial has costs:

### Living as if There's Always Tomorrow
- Putting off what matters
- "I'll do it later" becomes "I never did it"
- Wasted time on things that don't matter

### Wasted Conflict
- Grudges held for years
- Petty disputes that consume energy
- "I should've said I love you" at funerals

### Numbing and Distraction
- Endless scrolling, consuming, acquiring
- Anything to avoid the existential question
- A life lived on the surface

### Living for Others' Approval
- Choices made to please, not to fulfill
- A life that wasn't really yours
- Regrets on the deathbed

---

## Death as Teacher

Every spiritual tradition uses death as a teacher:

### Buddhism
Maranasati—death meditation. Reflecting on death clarifies what's worth doing with the time we have.

### Stoicism
Memento mori—remember death. The Stoics carried reminders of mortality to live with greater purpose.

### Christianity
"In the midst of life, we are in death." Death awareness calls for living rightly.

### Indigenous Traditions
Death as part of the cycle. Living in relationship with death, not denial of it.

**The wise have always known: death awareness serves life.**

## The Deathbed Test

A powerful exercise: Imagine yourself at the end of your life.

### Ask:
- What will matter then?
- What won't matter at all?
- What will you regret not doing?
- What will you be grateful you did?
- Who will you wish you'd loved better?
- What will you wish you'd released sooner?

### The Findings:
Studies of the dying reveal common regrets:
- "I wish I'd lived a life true to myself, not what others expected"
- "I wish I'd let myself be happier"
- "I wish I'd stayed in touch with friends"
- "I wish I'd expressed my feelings"
- "I wish I hadn't worked so hard"

**Notice: None of them are "I wish I'd been more cautious" or "I wish I'd played it safer."**

---

## Death and Authenticity

Death strips away pretense. When you know you'll die:
- Why live someone else's life?
- Why hold onto things that don't matter?
- Why not say what you really think?
- Why not do what you actually want?

### The Question Death Asks:
"Given that your time is limited, how do you want to spend it?"

This question cuts through the noise. It clarifies instantly.

## Death and Presence

Those who live with death awareness are more present.

### Why:
- The past is gone
- The future isn't guaranteed
- Only now is real
- Death makes each moment precious

### The Shift:
From "This moment doesn't matter; what matters is the future"
To "This moment is all there is; I will be fully here"

---

## Death and Love

Death clarifies love.

### What If:
- What if this was the last time you saw them?
- What if these were your last words?
- What if this was your final chance to connect?

### The Effect:
- Petty grievances dissolve
- What's unsaid gets said
- Presence replaces distraction
- Love comes to the foreground

---

## Not Morbid, But Alive

This isn't morbid preoccupation with death. It's the opposite.

### Morbid Death Obsession:
- Paralyzed by fear
- Constantly anxious
- Unable to enjoy life
- Focused on death, not life

### Conscious Death Awareness:
- Death as teacher, not terror
- Present because of mortality
- Living fully because time is limited
- Death in service of life

**The goal isn't to dwell on death—it's to let death inform living.**

---

## Key Concepts

- **Death Awareness:** Conscious recognition of mortality as a teacher for life.

- **The Deathbed Test:** Using the vantage point of the end to clarify what matters now.

- **Memento Mori:** "Remember death"—the ancient practice of keeping mortality in view.

- **Death and Presence:** How mortality awareness creates fuller presence in each moment.

- **Denial Costs:** What we lose by pretending death isn't real.
