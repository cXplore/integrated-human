## The Green Monster

Jealousy is one of the most painful emotions in relationships. It can consume you—the obsessive thoughts, the sick feeling in your stomach, the urge to check and control.

Most people experience jealousy as something happening to them, triggered by their partner. But jealousy is rarely just about what they're doing. It's about something much deeper.

---

## What Jealousy Actually Is

Jealousy appears to be about your partner's behavior. But underneath, it's about:

**Fear of loss.**
Losing them, losing the relationship, losing what you have.

**Fear of not being enough.**
Someone else is better. You don't measure up.

**Fear of abandonment.**
Being left. Being replaced. Being alone.

**Fear of betrayal.**
Being made a fool of. Having trust destroyed.

Jealousy is fear wearing a different mask. The partner is the trigger. The wound is yours.

---

## Healthy vs. Unhealthy Jealousy

Not all jealousy is the same:

**Healthy jealousy:**
- Occasional, mild discomfort
- Based on genuine concerns (actual warning signs)
- You can talk about it openly
- It passes relatively quickly
- Doesn't control your behavior

**Unhealthy jealousy:**
- Constant, intense, consuming
- Based on insecurity more than reality
- Creates conflict, control, or distance
- Drives obsessive thoughts and behaviors
- Damages the relationship

Everyone experiences some jealousy. The question is whether it's proportionate and manageable, or whether it's running your life.

---

## The Jealousy Spiral

Unhealthy jealousy often follows a pattern:

**1. Trigger**
Something happens—a glance, a text, an innocent interaction.

**2. Fear activation**
The wound gets touched. Fear of loss, not-enoughness, abandonment.

**3. Catastrophic thinking**
Your mind constructs worst-case scenarios. "They're definitely attracted to them."

**4. Emotional flooding**
Intense feelings—anxiety, anger, despair.

**5. Reactive behavior**
Checking, questioning, accusing, controlling.

**6. Relationship damage**
Your partner feels untrusted. Distance increases.

**7. Increased insecurity**
The distance confirms your fears. The cycle continues.

Understanding the spiral helps you interrupt it.

## Jealousy Is Information

Like all emotions, jealousy contains information. The question is: what's it telling you?

**Sometimes it's pointing to a real concern:**
- Your partner is actually behaving inappropriately
- There are genuine red flags to address
- Your boundaries are being violated

**Often it's pointing to your own wounds:**
- Old abandonment fears being triggered
- Not-enoughness beliefs surfacing
- Past betrayals creating present hypervigilance

The work is distinguishing between external reality (real concern) and internal reality (your patterns). Both are valid—but they require different responses.

---

## What Makes Jealousy Worse

**Certainty-seeking.**
Trying to control all variables, eliminate all threats. This is impossible and exhausting.

**Reassurance-seeking.**
Constantly needing to hear you're loved, they're not interested in anyone else. The relief never lasts.

**Checking behaviors.**
Phones, emails, social media. This erodes trust and feeds the obsession.

**Comparison.**
Looking at others and measuring yourself against them.

**Isolation.**
Trying to limit your partner's contact with others. This damages the relationship.

These behaviors feel like solutions but actually make jealousy worse.

---

## Key Concepts

- **Jealousy is fear in disguise.** Fear of loss, not-enoughness, abandonment.

- **Healthy jealousy is different from unhealthy.** Proportionate vs. consuming.

- **Jealousy is information.** Sometimes about real concerns, often about your wounds.

- **The trigger is present; the wound is old.** Your partner activates fears that predate them.
