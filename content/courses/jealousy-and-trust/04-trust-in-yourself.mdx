## A Different Kind of Trust

When we talk about trust in relationships, we usually mean trusting your partner. But there's a more fundamental trust that comes first:

**Trust in yourself.**

Trust that no matter what happens—even worst-case scenarios—you'll be okay. You'll survive. You'll handle it.

This kind of trust changes everything.

---

## Why Self-Trust Matters

When you trust yourself:

**You can tolerate uncertainty.**
You don't need to know exactly what your partner is doing every moment. You can handle not knowing.

**Jealousy loses its power.**
Even if the worst happened, you'd be okay. So the fear isn't as urgent.

**You're less controlling.**
You don't need to prevent all possible bad outcomes because you know you can handle outcomes.

**You're more attractive.**
Security is attractive. Desperation is not.

**You can love freely.**
Without clinging, without fear dominating.

Self-trust is the foundation of secure relating.

---

## What Self-Trust Actually Is

Trust in yourself means:

**You can handle your emotions.**
Even painful ones. They won't destroy you.

**You can handle loss.**
It would hurt. But you'd survive. You'd rebuild.

**You're capable of recovery.**
You've been through hard things before. You got through.

**Your worth isn't dependent on this relationship.**
If it ends, you're still worthy. You're still whole.

**You can face reality.**
Whatever it is, you can see it and deal with it.

This isn't arrogance or denial. It's a deep knowing that you're resilient.

---

## Where Self-Trust Goes Missing

Self-trust erodes when:

**Past experiences shattered you.**
Betrayal, loss, or abandonment that you didn't fully recover from.

**You believe you can't handle pain.**
You've learned to avoid rather than face.

**Your identity is fused with the relationship.**
Without them, you don't know who you are.

**You've never tested yourself.**
You don't know you can handle difficulty because you've always avoided it.

Without self-trust, you need to control everything to feel safe—because you don't trust yourself to handle what might happen.

## Building Self-Trust

Self-trust is built through:

**Facing what you've avoided.**
Each time you feel something difficult and survive, you prove to yourself you can.

**Remembering your history.**
You've been through hard things before. You're still here.

**Developing your capacity.**
Skills for emotional regulation, support systems, coping strategies.

**Knowing your worth isn't conditional.**
On this relationship, on anyone's approval.

**Testing yourself.**
Taking on challenges, facing fears. Discovering what you're capable of.

---

## The Worst-Case Scenario Exercise

This is counterintuitive, but facing your worst fear can reduce its power.

**Ask yourself:**
- What exactly am I afraid will happen?
- If it happened, then what?
- And then what?
- How would I cope?
- Who would help me?
- What would I do to rebuild?

Usually, by the time you follow the fear to its conclusion, you realize: "I'd survive. It would hurt, but I'd get through it."

When you know you can handle the worst, the fear of it decreases.

---

## Self-Trust and Relationships

With self-trust:

**You can love without clinging.**
Because losing them, while painful, wouldn't destroy you.

**You can be present.**
Instead of constantly guarding against future pain.

**You can give them freedom.**
Because their choices don't determine your worth or survival.

**You're more secure.**
And security is attractive.

**You can trust them (or not) for real.**
Your choice to trust comes from assessment, not desperation.

Self-trust is the foundation on which trust in others can be built.

---

## Key Concepts

- **Self-trust is foundational.** Trust in yourself enables trust in others.

- **It means knowing you'll be okay** no matter what happens.

- **It's built through facing**, not avoiding, difficult experiences.

- **When you trust yourself, jealousy loses power.** You don't need to prevent every possible outcome.
