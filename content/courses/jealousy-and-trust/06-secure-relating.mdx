## A Different Foundation

Most relationships operate from some degree of fear:
- Fear of abandonment
- Fear of not being enough
- Fear of betrayal
- Fear of loss

What would it be like to relate from security instead?

---

## What Secure Relating Looks Like

**When you relate from security:**

You're present rather than vigilant. You enjoy the moment rather than scanning for threats.

You give freedom rather than control. You trust them to make good choices without monitoring.

You express rather than suppress. You can name jealousy when it arises without drama.

You trust yourself to handle anything. Even if the worst happens, you know you'll be okay.

You can be separate. Your partner can have their own life, friends, interests—and that's good.

You can be together. Without merging, without losing yourself.

---

## Secure Attachment in Action

Secure attachment isn't the absence of difficult emotions. It's how you handle them:

**When jealousy arises:**
You notice it, feel it, and address it directly rather than acting out.

**When conflicts happen:**
You work through them rather than running or attacking.

**When you feel insecure:**
You can ask for reassurance without it being desperate or constant.

**When they need space:**
You can give it without falling apart.

**When trust is tested:**
You can address it openly rather than spiraling or pretending.

Security isn't a state of no problems. It's a way of being that can handle problems.

---

## From Fear to Security

Moving from fear-based relating to secure relating involves:

**Healing your wounds.**
The abandonment, not-enoughness, and attachment injuries that drive fear.

**Building self-trust.**
Knowing you'll be okay no matter what.

**Developing secure patterns.**
Replacing reactive behaviors with responsive ones.

**Choosing partners wisely.**
People who are capable of secure relating themselves.

**Practice.**
Security is built through consistent practice, not one insight.

## The Paradox of Control

Here's a paradox:

**The more you try to control**, the less secure you feel. Because control never provides lasting security. There's always something else to worry about.

**The less you try to control**, the more secure you can feel. Because security comes from trusting yourself, not from eliminating all threats.

Letting go of control feels risky. But it's actually the path to security.

---

## What Secure Love Feels Like

Secure love feels like:

- Being able to relax
- Not constantly monitoring
- Enjoying rather than worrying
- Trust that doesn't require certainty
- Freedom within commitment
- Knowing you're chosen, not trapped
- Conflict that leads to growth, not damage
- Two whole people, together by choice

It's not the intensity of anxious attachment. It's not the distance of avoidant attachment. It's something calmer, steadier, deeper.

## The Path Forward

Working with jealousy and building trust is ongoing work:

- Continue healing your wounds
- Practice pausing when triggered
- Build your self-trust through facing challenges
- Develop discernment about who to trust
- Cultivate security as a practice

Jealousy won't disappear completely. But it can become manageable. And trust—in yourself and others—can become your foundation.

---

## Key Concepts

- **Secure relating is possible.** It's a practice, not a fixed state.

- **Security comes from self-trust**, not from controlling everything.

- **Letting go of control paradoxically creates security.**

- **Secure love is calm, not anxious.** Present, not vigilant.

---

*The goal isn't to never feel jealousy. It's to feel it without letting it run your life—and to build a foundation of trust that can hold it.*
