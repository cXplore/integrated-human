## Welcome to The Integration Path

You're here because something in you knows there's more.

More presence. More aliveness. More capacity. More of YOU available for your life.

This 8-week journey is designed to help you become more integrated—more whole. Not by adding anything new, but by bringing together what's already there, healing what's fragmented, and developing what's underdeveloped.

**Integration is the path to living as your full self.**

---

## What Is Integration?

Integration isn't a destination—it's a process of becoming more whole.

### The Integrated Person:

- **Knows themselves** — Aware of patterns, shadows, strengths, wounds
- **Can regulate** — Nervous system flexible, emotions accessible but not overwhelming
- **Is embodied** — Present in their body, not just their head
- **Relates well** — Can connect deeply while maintaining self
- **Has meaning** — Knows what matters and lives accordingly
- **Is adaptive** — Can meet life's challenges without fragmenting

### The Fragmented Person:

- **Cut off from parts of themselves** — Shadow, body, emotions exiled
- **Reactive rather than responsive** — Nervous system runs the show
- **Living in their head** — Disconnected from body wisdom
- **Struggles in relationships** — Patterns repeat, intimacy difficult
- **Lacks direction** — Unclear values, borrowed meaning
- **Rigid or chaotic** — Can't adapt to life's demands

**Most of us are somewhere in between—integrated in some areas, fragmented in others.**

## The Four Pillars

This course works with four interconnected domains:

### Mind
- Your psychology, patterns, beliefs
- Shadow material and unconscious dynamics
- How you think, interpret, make meaning
- Emotional capacity and intelligence

### Body
- Your nervous system and regulation
- Physical health and embodiment
- Somatic experience and sensation
- The body as foundation

### Soul
- Your deeper self beyond personality
- Purpose, meaning, values
- Spiritual dimension (however you understand it)
- What makes life worth living

### Relationships
- How you connect with others
- Attachment patterns
- Intimacy, conflict, repair
- Your relational self

**These aren't separate—they're interconnected.** Nervous system dysregulation affects relationships. Shadow material shapes meaning. Body holds emotions. Everything connects.

## Exercise: The Integration Map

This week's major exercise: mapping where you are across all four pillars.

### Part 1: Self-Assessment

Rate yourself 1-10 in each area (1 = major struggle, 10 = fully developed):

**MIND**
- Self-awareness (knowing your patterns, triggers, tendencies): ___
- Shadow integration (relationship with disowned parts): ___
- Emotional intelligence (recognizing and working with emotions): ___
- Mental clarity (clear thinking, not hijacked by patterns): ___

**BODY**
- Nervous system regulation (ability to calm/energize when needed): ___
- Embodiment (present in body, not just head): ___
- Physical health (energy, vitality, body function): ___
- Somatic awareness (feeling sensations, body signals): ___

**SOUL**
- Sense of meaning (life feels purposeful): ___
- Connection to values (know what matters, live accordingly): ___
- Presence (ability to be here now): ___
- Depth (access to something beyond surface life): ___

**RELATIONSHIPS**
- Attachment security (comfortable with intimacy and independence): ___
- Communication (express needs, hear others): ___
- Conflict capacity (handle disagreement without destruction): ___
- Depth of connection (truly known by others): ___

### Part 2: Identify Patterns

Look at your scores:

**Strongest pillar:** Which area has your highest scores?

**Weakest pillar:** Which area has your lowest scores?

**Pattern:** How do your strengths and weaknesses connect? (Often our strengths compensate for weaknesses—e.g., strong Mind to avoid Body)

### Part 3: Personal History

For each pillar, briefly answer:

**Mind:** What shaped your psychological patterns? What was modeled?

**Body:** What was your relationship with your body growing up? How was regulation taught (or not)?

**Soul:** What gave life meaning in your family? What was the spiritual/existential atmosphere?

**Relationships:** What attachment patterns did you learn? What was modeled?

### Part 4: Current Impact

**Where does fragmentation cost you most right now?**

- In your health?
- In your relationships?
- In your work?
- In your inner peace?
- In your sense of meaning?

### Part 5: Intention Setting

Complete these statements:

"By the end of these 8 weeks, I want to..."

"The part of myself I most want to integrate is..."

"I'm willing to be honest about..."

"I commit to this process by..."

## Daily Practices: Week 1

Do these each day this week:

### Morning (5-10 min)

**Body Scan + State Check**
1. Upon waking, before phone, lie still for 2 minutes
2. Scan from head to feet—what do you notice?
3. Ask: What state is my nervous system in? (Calm/Activated/Shutdown)
4. Ask: What emotions are present?
5. Set intention: "Today I will notice..."

### Throughout Day

**Pillar Check-Ins**
Set 3 reminders to ask:
- Mind: What am I thinking? What's running?
- Body: What do I feel physically? What state am I in?
- Soul: Am I present? Does this moment have meaning?
- Relationships: How am I connecting (or not) with others?

Just notice. Don't try to change anything yet.

### Evening (5-10 min)

**Day Review**
In your journal:
1. What did I notice about myself today?
2. Which pillar showed up most? Least?
3. Where was I integrated? Where fragmented?
4. What wants attention?
