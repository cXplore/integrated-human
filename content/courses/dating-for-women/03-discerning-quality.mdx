## The Core Skill

As a woman in dating, you're often in the position of choosing—from the people who pursue you, express interest, or show up in your life.

The question isn't just "is there chemistry?" It's: **is this person actually worth your time?**

Discernment is the skill of recognizing quality—and recognizing its absence.

---

## Why This Matters

Many women waste years on:
- Men who seemed great at first but weren't
- Men who showed warning signs they ignored
- Men who were never going to give them what they wanted
- Men they knew weren't right but couldn't leave

Developing discernment saves you time, heartache, and the slow erosion of self-trust that comes from repeatedly ignoring your instincts.

---

## Red Flags

Warning signs that suggest a man may not be a good partner:

### Behavior Red Flags

**Words don't match actions.**
He says he wants a relationship but doesn't act like it. Talk is easy. Watch what he does.

**Inconsistent effort.**
Hot and cold. Intense then distant. You never know where you stand.

**Disrespects boundaries.**
When you say no, he pushes. Doesn't respect limits you set.

**Love bombing.**
Too much too soon. Excessive flattery, premature intensity. This often precedes manipulation.

**No accountability.**
Nothing is ever his fault. He blames exes, circumstances, you—never himself.

**Controlling behavior.**
Wants to know where you are, who you're with. Isolates you from friends and family.

### Character Red Flags

**Treats others poorly.**
How he treats waiters, his mother, strangers. Watch carefully.

**Chronic victim.**
The world is against him. Nothing goes right. Everyone has wronged him.

**Dishonesty.**
Even "small" lies indicate a pattern. Trust is eroded by degrees.

**No growth.**
Same problems for years with no change. Doesn't reflect or learn.

**Anger issues.**
Quick to rage. Makes you walk on eggshells.

---

## Green Flags

Signs that suggest a man may be worth your time:

### Behavior Green Flags

**Words match actions.**
He does what he says. You can predict him.

**Consistent.**
Shows up regularly. Reliable in mood, attention, effort.

**Respects boundaries.**
When you say no, he respects it. Doesn't push.

**Healthy pace.**
Doesn't rush. Let's things unfold naturally.

**Takes responsibility.**
Owns his mistakes. Apologizes genuinely.

### Character Green Flags

**Treats everyone well.**
Kindness isn't just for you—it's who he is.

**Has purpose.**
His life has direction. He's building something.

**Self-aware.**
Knows his strengths and weaknesses. Reflects on himself.

**Emotionally available.**
Can talk about feelings. Present in difficult conversations.

**Stable foundation.**
Friendships, family, work. A life that functions.

## The Danger of Potential

One of the biggest traps: falling for potential instead of reality.

**"He could be great if..."**
- If he just stopped drinking
- If he dealt with his issues
- If he had more time
- If things were different

Potential is not the same as reality. You're dating who he is now, not who he might become. And you're not his therapist.

**The rule:** Date the person in front of you, not the person you hope he'll become.

---

## Time Reveals

You can't fully assess someone on date one. Character reveals itself over time, especially when:

- Things get hard
- You disagree
- His words and actions have time to match up or not
- The new relationship excitement fades
- Real life circumstances arise

This is why a healthy pace matters. Rushing into commitment doesn't give discernment time to work.

---

## Trust Your Gut

Your intuition is a powerful tool—if you listen to it.

**Signs your gut is warning you:**
- A vague sense of unease
- Something feels "off" but you can't name it
- Your body is tense around him
- You find yourself making excuses for his behavior
- You're hoping rather than trusting

**What overrides your gut:**
- Really wanting it to work
- Chemistry that clouds judgment
- Fear of being alone
- Hope that you're wrong
- Investment of time and emotion

Your gut isn't always right. But when it's been warning you repeatedly, it's usually worth listening to.

---

## When Red Flags Appear

What to do when you see a red flag:

**1. Don't explain it away.**
Notice your mind's tendency to excuse. "He was stressed." "He didn't mean it."

**2. Watch for patterns.**
One incident might be a fluke. Repeated incidents are a pattern.

**3. Trust what you observe.**
Believe behavior, not promises to change.

**4. Be willing to leave.**
The sooner you act on red flags, the less invested you are.

**5. Learn from it.**
Each experience sharpens your discernment for next time.

---

## Key Concepts

- **Discernment is learned.** You get better at recognizing quality through experience and awareness.

- **Red flags are almost always visible.** The question is whether you're willing to see and act on them.

- **Time reveals character.** Don't rush the assessment.

- **Trust your gut.** It knows things your mind hasn't figured out yet.
