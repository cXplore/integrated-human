## Living From the Neck Up

Many of us are walking heads. We live in our thoughts, our plans, our worries. The body is just a vehicle that carries the head around.

**We're not really in our bodies. We're floating somewhere above them.**

This course is about coming home—learning to inhabit the body you've always had but rarely occupied.

---

## What Is Embodiment?

Embodiment is more than having a body. It's living IN and THROUGH your body.

### An Embodied Person:
- Feels what's happening in their body
- Uses body sensations as information
- Is present in physical experience
- Has access to the body's wisdom
- Lives in the body, not above it

### A Disembodied Person:
- Lives mostly in thoughts
- Numb to physical sensations
- Surprised by body signals (only notices when ill)
- Makes decisions purely mentally
- Uses body only for function

**Most modern people lean heavily disembodied.**

---

## How We Become Disconnected

### Trauma
When overwhelming things happen, we leave the body to survive. The body holds pain we couldn't process. Disconnection becomes protection.

### Chronic Stress
Constant stress makes the body uncomfortable. We escape to the mind to avoid feeling the tension, the racing heart, the clenched jaw.

### Cultural Conditioning
Western culture values thinking over feeling. We're trained to be productive, not present. The body is for exercise, not wisdom.

### Technology
Screens pull attention out of physical reality. Hours pass without body awareness. The virtual replaces the visceral.

### Shame
If the body was criticized, objectified, or violated, disconnection becomes relief. Better not to feel what's there.

## The Cost of Disconnection

Living disconnected from the body has real costs:

### Missed Information
The body constantly gives information:
- Emotions register physically before mentally
- Intuition speaks through body sensation
- Danger signals come as physical responses
- Needs announce themselves somatically

**When you're not in your body, you miss all of this.**

### Emotional Regulation
Emotions are physical experiences. If you can't feel your body, you can't process emotions well. They either overwhelm or get suppressed.

### Health
Ignoring body signals leads to:
- Chronic tension becoming injury
- Stress becoming disease
- Fatigue ignored until burnout
- Symptoms missed until serious

### Presence
You can't be fully present without body awareness. The body is your anchor to now. Without it, you float in past and future.

### Connection
Deep connection with others requires body presence. Attunement, empathy, intimacy—all are embodied experiences.

## Interoception

Interoception is your ability to sense internal body states:
- Heart rate
- Breathing
- Temperature
- Hunger/fullness
- Muscle tension
- Gut sensations
- Energy levels

### Low Interoception:
- Can't feel subtle body signals
- Needs extremes to register
- Body is "quiet" or "numb"
- Difficulty identifying emotions

### High Interoception:
- Feels subtle physical changes
- Notices body shifts early
- Body is "alive" with sensation
- Emotions felt as body states

**Interoception can be developed. That's what this course teaches.**

---

## The Body Knows

The body holds wisdom the mind doesn't have.

### Body Wisdom Includes:
- Felt sense of "yes" or "no"
- Gut feelings about people/situations
- Physical signs of truth
- Intuitive knowing
- Emotional intelligence

### How to Access It:
1. Be in the body (embodiment)
2. Feel what's there (interoception)
3. Trust and follow the signals

**You already have this wisdom. You just need to learn to listen.**

## Coming Home

Embodiment is a homecoming. It's returning to a place you've always been but rarely inhabited.

### What Changes with Embodiment:
- More presence in moments
- Better emotional regulation
- Clearer intuition
- Deeper relationships
- Better health awareness
- More aliveness

### What It Requires:
- Slowing down
- Turning attention inward
- Tolerating what you find
- Regular practice
- Patience with the process

---

## The Path

This course will teach you:

1. **Basic Body Awareness** - Learning to feel what's there
2. **Grounding Practices** - Coming out of the head into the body
3. **Body as Resource** - Using somatic awareness for guidance
4. **Daily Practice** - Maintaining embodiment in regular life

These are fundamental skills. Once learned, they support everything else—emotional work, relationships, spiritual practice, healing.

## Key Concepts

- **Embodiment:** Living in and through your body, not just in your head.

- **Interoception:** The ability to sense internal body states.

- **Disconnection:** The state of living above or outside the body.

- **Body Wisdom:** The knowing available through physical sensation and felt sense.

- **Homecoming:** Returning to inhabit the body you've always had.
