## The Basics of Emotional Regulation

### What Regulation Is

Emotional regulation means:
- Influencing which emotions you have
- When you have them
- How you experience them
- How you express them

### What It's Not

Regulation is not:
- Suppression (pushing emotions down)
- Elimination (having no emotions)
- Control (white-knuckling through)
- Denial (pretending you don't feel)

### The Goal

Healthy regulation means:
- Experiencing emotions fully
- Not being controlled by them
- Choosing how to respond
- Processing emotions effectively
- Returning to baseline

---

## Simple Regulation Techniques

### 1. The Pause

When emotions rise:
- Stop before reacting
- Take one breath
- Create a moment of space
- Then choose your response

This simple pause is powerful—it interrupts the automatic trigger-reaction cycle.

### 2. Deep Breathing

Slow breathing calms the nervous system:
- Breathe in for 4 counts
- Hold for 4 counts
- Breathe out for 6 counts
- Repeat 3-5 times

The extended exhale activates your relaxation response.

### 3. Name It to Tame It

When you feel strong emotion:
- Put a name to it: "I notice I'm feeling angry"
- This engages your prefrontal cortex
- Reduces emotional intensity
- Creates distance from the feeling

### 4. Grounding

When emotions overwhelm:
- Feel your feet on the floor
- Notice 5 things you can see
- Touch something with texture
- Bring yourself to present moment

## Building Daily Habits

### Morning Check-In

Start each day with:
- How am I feeling right now?
- What emotions are present?
- What do I need today?
- What might trigger me today?

### Throughout the Day

Regular practices:
- Brief pause before responding
- Periodic emotion check-ins
- Noticing body sensations
- Catching reactions early

### Evening Reflection

End each day with:
- What emotions did I experience today?
- What triggered me?
- How did I respond?
- What could I do differently?

## When Emotions Get Intense

### Signs of Overwhelm

Watch for:
- Feeling out of control
- Strong physical sensations
- Racing thoughts
- Urge to act impulsively
- Tunnel vision

### The Intensity Dial

Think of emotion intensity as 1-10:
- 1-3: Easily manageable
- 4-6: Requires attention
- 7-8: Needs active regulation
- 9-10: Need to stop and calm first

### High Intensity Strategy

When emotions are 7+:
1. Stop what you're doing
2. Remove yourself if possible
3. Use calming techniques (breathing, grounding)
4. Wait until intensity drops to 5-6
5. Then address the situation

Don't try to process or respond when intensity is too high.

- **Grounding:** Present-moment awareness for overwhelm

- **High intensity:** Stop, calm first, then respond
