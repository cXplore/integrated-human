## The Question That Won't Go Away

Every man feels it at some point: "What am I here for? What's the point?"

Purpose isn't optional. Without it, achievement feels empty, success feels hollow, and life lacks meaning.

**A man without purpose drifts. A man with purpose has direction, energy, and a reason to sacrifice.**

---

## Why Purpose Matters

### What Purpose Provides:
- Direction for decisions
- Energy to persist
- Meaning in difficulty
- Framework for priorities
- Reason to develop yourself
- Contribution beyond self

### Without Purpose:
- Achievement without fulfillment
- Success that feels empty
- Chronic restlessness
- Searching without finding
- Drifting through life

## Clarifying Purpose

### What Purpose Is:
- Your unique contribution
- What you're here to give
- The intersection of gifts, passion, and need
- What calls you forward

### What Purpose Is Not:
- A job title
- Achievement or success
- Someone else's expectation
- Static and never changing

### Two Levels:
**Core Purpose:** The fundamental way you serve (may remain constant)
**Expression:** How it shows up in different life phases (changes)

---

## The Elements of Purpose

### 1. Your Gifts
What are you naturally good at? What comes easily that others struggle with?

### 2. Your Passions
What lights you up? What would you do even if no one paid you?

### 3. World Need
What does the world need that you can provide? Where is there suffering you could address?

### 4. Economic Viability
Can you make a living doing this? (Important but not primary)

### The Intersection:
Purpose lives where these overlap.

## Purpose Discovery Methods

### Reflect on Flow:
When do you lose track of time? What were you doing during your best moments?

### Ask Others:
What do people come to you for? What do they say you're great at?

### Recall Childhood:
What did you love before you learned to perform? What called you?

### Notice Anger:
What wrongs make you angry? What injustice moves you? Purpose often hides in outrage.

### Try Things:
Purpose is discovered in action, not just reflection. Experiment.

### Death Meditation:
What would you regret NOT having done? What would you want to be remembered for?

## From Purpose to Mission

### Purpose:
The overarching why—your fundamental reason for being.

### Mission:
The current expression—what you're doing about it now.

### Example:
- Purpose: Help people heal
- Mission: Build a therapy practice for trauma survivors

### Mission Changes:
Your mission may change over life phases. Purpose often remains more stable.

---

## Crafting Your Purpose Statement

### Format:
"I exist to [verb] [what] for [whom] so that [impact]."

### Examples:
- "I exist to create beauty that awakens people to what matters."
- "I exist to protect and provide for my family and community so they can thrive."
- "I exist to teach men to become fully alive."

### Keep It:
- Simple enough to remember
- Specific enough to guide
- Inspiring enough to motivate

## Obstacles to Purpose

### Fear of Failure:
What if you try and fail? Better to not try? This keeps you stuck.

### Fear of Success:
What if it works? Then what? Identity threat.

### Others' Expectations:
Living someone else's purpose. Family, culture, society.

### Not Knowing:
Waiting until you're "sure." Certainty rarely comes first.

### Unworthiness:
"Who am I to...?" Imposter feelings.

### Comfort:
Purpose requires sacrifice. Comfort is easier.

## Living Your Purpose

### Purpose Isn't Just Known:
It's lived. Every day. In choices and actions.

### Daily Purpose Alignment:
- Does today's schedule reflect my purpose?
- Am I moving toward or away from what matters?
- What one action serves my purpose today?

### Purpose-Driven Decisions:
When faced with choices, which option serves purpose?

### Saying No:
Purpose requires saying no to what doesn't serve it.

---

## Purpose and Sacrifice

### The Truth:
Purpose requires sacrifice. You can't have everything AND live your purpose.

### What You May Sacrifice:
- Comfort
- Others' approval
- Other paths not taken
- Easy money
- Conventional success

### The Exchange:
You trade these for meaning, fulfillment, and a life that matters.

## When Purpose Is Unclear

### It's Okay:
Not everyone has clear purpose immediately. It often emerges.

### What to Do:
- Keep exploring
- Take action (clarity comes from doing)
- Follow interest and energy
- Pay attention to what calls you
- Trust the process

### Avoid:
- Paralysis waiting for certainty
- Letting lack of clarity become excuse for inaction
- Chronic seeking without trying

---

## Purpose in Stages of Life

### Young Adulthood:
Exploration, trying things, building skills.

### Middle Adulthood:
Execution, contribution, building legacy.

### Later Life:
Wisdom, mentoring, passing on.

### Each Stage:
Purpose expression changes. Core purpose may remain.
