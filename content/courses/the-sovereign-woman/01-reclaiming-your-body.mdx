## Welcome to The Sovereign Woman

This is not a course about becoming someone else. It's about returning to yourself—the self that existed before you learned to shrink, perform, please, and doubt.

Sovereignty begins in the body. A body you may have learned to distrust, control, criticize, and abandon.

**Coming home to your body is the foundation of everything else.**

---

## The Body You Were Given

### What Happened:
From the earliest age, you received messages about your body:

- Too much or not enough
- Wrong shape, wrong size
- Something to control, not trust
- A problem to solve
- Something for others to evaluate
- Source of danger and vulnerability

### The Result:
A complicated, often painful relationship with your own flesh. A body you live in but don't fully inhabit.

## How We Leave Our Bodies

### Common Patterns:

**Dissociation:**
Checking out, floating above, not being "in" the body.

**Hypervigilance:**
Watching the body from outside, evaluating, judging.

**Numbing:**
Not feeling, using food, substances, or activity to not feel.

**Control:**
Rigidly managing the body—food, exercise, appearance—from fear.

**Objectification:**
Seeing the body as thing, not self. How it looks, not how it feels.

## The Body as Home

### The Shift:
From body as object → body as self
From body as problem → body as ally
From body as enemy → body as home

### What This Means:
- Living inside your body, not outside it
- Feeling sensation without judgment
- Trusting body signals
- Being at home in your own skin

## What Bodies Actually Want

### They Want:
- To be felt, not judged
- To move in ways that feel good
- Nourishment without punishment
- Rest when tired
- Pleasure without shame
- To be listened to

### They Don't Want:
- Constant criticism
- Control from fear
- To be ignored
- To be managed like a problem
- Punishment disguised as discipline

## Beginning to Return

### Practices for Coming Home:

**Sensation Tracking:**
Simply notice what you feel. Temperature, pressure, tingling, heaviness. No judgment, just awareness.

**Body Scan:**
Move attention slowly through your body. Notice each area. Arrive.

**Gentle Touch:**
Hands on heart, belly, face. Touching yourself with tenderness.

**Breath Awareness:**
Feel breath moving in and out. Let it bring you here.

**Pleasure:**
Small pleasures—warmth, texture, taste—without guilt.

## The Body as Source of Wisdom

### What Many Women Have Lost:
Connection to body wisdom—intuition, gut knowing, felt sense.

### What the Body Knows:
- What's true for you
- What's safe and unsafe
- What you want
- What you need
- When something's off

### Reclaiming:
Learning to listen again. Trusting what you feel.

---

## Body Image vs. Body Experience

### The Difference:

**Body Image:**
How you THINK you look. From outside. Comparative.

**Body Experience:**
How you FEEL in your body. From inside. Direct.

### The Shift:
From obsession with image → cultivation of experience.

### This Is Revolutionary:
In a culture that wants you focused on how you look, choosing to focus on how you feel is an act of rebellion.
