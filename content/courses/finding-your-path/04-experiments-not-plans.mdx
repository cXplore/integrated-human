## The Trap of Planning

When you're lost, planning can become a trap.

You try to figure it all out before you start:
- The perfect career
- The complete life plan
- The certain path
- The guaranteed outcome

**But you can't think your way to your path. You have to walk it.**

---

## Why Planning Doesn't Work Here

### You Can't Know Until You Try
You don't know if something fits until you experience it. No amount of research replaces trying.

### Information Is Incomplete
The most important information only comes through action. You're missing data that only doing provides.

### You're Not Static
You change through the process. What you want now may not be what you want after trying things.

### The Path Isn't Linear
Your path won't be a straight line. It will zigzag through experiments.

---

## The Experiment Mindset

Instead of planning the whole path, run experiments:

### What Is an Experiment?
- A small, time-limited trial
- Low risk, high information
- Done to learn, not to commit
- Failure is valuable data

### Experiment vs. Commitment:
- **Commitment:** "I'm going to be a therapist" (huge decision, high stakes)
- **Experiment:** "I'll volunteer at a counseling center for two months" (small, reversible, informative)

## Designing Good Experiments

### Small:
- Not life-altering
- Recoverable if it doesn't work
- Hours or weeks, not years

### Time-Limited:
- Clear endpoint
- "I'll try this for one month"
- Evaluation built in

### Information-Rich:
- Will teach you something important
- Addresses a key unknown
- Real experience, not just thinking

### Low-Stakes:
- Failure isn't catastrophic
- You can change course
- No huge investment required

---

## Examples of Experiments

### Career Exploration:
- Shadow someone in a field that interests you
- Take a freelance project in a new area
- Volunteer related to your curiosity
- Take a class or workshop
- Do a short internship or trial

### Lifestyle Experiments:
- Try a different morning routine for a month
- Spend a week in a city you're considering
- Take a mini-retirement or sabbatical
- Live below your means to see what matters

### Creative/Personal:
- Commit to a practice for 30 days
- Share work publicly for a limited time
- Join a community you're curious about
- Start a small project, not a career

## Learning from Experiments

### After Each Experiment, Ask:
- What did I learn?
- Did this give me energy or drain me?
- Do I want more of this or less?
- What surprised me?
- What's the next experiment this suggests?

### Failure Is Data:
A "failed" experiment that shows you what you don't want is valuable. You've eliminated a path. That's progress.

---

## The Minimum Viable Step

When even experiments feel too big, ask:
**What's the smallest possible step?**

### Examples:
- Can't try a new career? Read one book about it.
- Can't move to a new city? Visit for a weekend.
- Can't start a business? Talk to one person who has.
- Can't commit to art? Draw for 10 minutes a day.

**The smallest step still moves you forward.**

## Running Multiple Experiments

You don't have to bet on one path. You can:

### Parallel Experiments:
- Try multiple things at once
- See what sticks
- Let things compete for your energy

### Sequential Experiments:
- Try one thing
- Learn from it
- Let it point to the next

### Portfolio Approach:
- Maintain several interests
- See which develop
- Don't put all eggs in one basket

---

## When Action Feels Impossible

Sometimes even small experiments feel overwhelming.

### Check For:
- Depression or burnout (address those first)
- Fear (Module 5 covers this)
- Perfectionism (demanding certainty before acting)
- Low energy (tend to your foundation)

### Remember:
- Action doesn't require feeling ready
- You can act scared
- Imperfect action beats perfect planning
- You're allowed to try things that don't work

## The Path Reveals Itself

Here's the truth: You can't see your whole path from where you stand.

The path reveals itself through walking. Each step shows you the next. Experiments generate the information you need.

**You don't need to know where you're going. You just need to take the next step.**

---

## Key Concepts

- **Experiments Over Plans:** Small, reversible trials rather than big commitments.

- **Information Through Action:** You can't think your way to clarity; you have to try things.

- **Failure as Data:** Experiments that don't work still teach you something valuable.

- **Minimum Viable Step:** The smallest possible action that moves you forward.

- **The Path Reveals Itself:** You can't see the whole path before walking; each step shows the next.
