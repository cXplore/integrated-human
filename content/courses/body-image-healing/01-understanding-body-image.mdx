## More Than a Reflection

Body image isn't about how your body actually looks. It's about how you perceive, think, and feel about your body.

Two people with identical bodies can have completely different body image experiences. One might feel comfortable and at home. The other might feel constant distress.

Understanding what body image actually is—and isn't—is the first step toward healing it.

---

## What Body Image Is

### The Four Dimensions

Body image has multiple components:

**Perceptual:**
- How you see your body
- Often doesn't match reality
- Can distort size, shape, features
- Changes based on mood and context

**Affective:**
- How you feel about your body
- Satisfaction, dissatisfaction
- Comfort, discomfort
- Shame, pride, neutrality

**Cognitive:**
- What you think about your body
- Beliefs about your appearance
- Assumptions about how others see you
- Stories you tell yourself

**Behavioral:**
- What you do because of body image
- Avoidance behaviors
- Checking behaviors
- Eating and exercise choices
- What you wear, where you go

All four dimensions interact. A thought triggers a feeling, which affects perception, which drives behavior.

---

## What Body Image Isn't

### Not Your Actual Body

Your body and your body image are different things:
- People in "ideal" bodies can have terrible body image
- People in non-conforming bodies can feel perfectly fine
- Changing your body doesn't automatically change how you feel about it

Many people discover that reaching their "goal body" doesn't bring the peace they expected.

### Not Fixed

Body image:
- Fluctuates day to day, even hour to hour
- Can improve or worsen over time
- Is shaped by experiences and environment
- Can be actively changed

If you've always struggled with body image, it doesn't mean you always will.

### Not Just a "Women's Issue"

Body image struggles affect:
- Men (often underreported)
- Non-binary individuals
- People of all ages
- People across all body types

The specific concerns may differ, but the underlying struggle is universal.

---

## How Body Image Develops

### Early Experiences

Body image begins forming in childhood:
- Comments from family members
- Comparisons to siblings or peers
- Early experiences of the body (illness, ability, appearance)
- Messages about food and eating
- Physical affection or lack thereof

Children absorb messages about bodies before they can evaluate them critically.

### Cultural Programming

Culture shapes body ideals:
- Media images
- Social media
- Fashion and beauty industries
- Cultural beauty standards
- Health and fitness messaging

You didn't choose what body type to value. You absorbed it from your environment.

### Personal History

Specific experiences shape body image:
- Bullying or teasing about appearance
- Sexual harassment or assault
- Athletic or performance contexts
- Medical experiences
- Significant comments (positive or negative)

Sometimes a single comment can alter body image for years.

### Ongoing Reinforcement

Body image continues to be shaped:
- Daily media exposure
- Conversations about bodies
- Health messaging
- Aging and body changes
- Life transitions

It's not just early experiences—current environment matters too.

## The Body Image Spectrum

### From Hatred to Love

Body image exists on a spectrum:

**Body hatred:**
- Active disgust and shame
- Constant preoccupation
- Avoidance and hiding
- Self-harm or punishment

**Body dissatisfaction:**
- Ongoing discomfort
- Desire to change
- Comparison and criticism
- Conditional acceptance

**Body neutrality:**
- Neither love nor hate
- Functional relationship
- Less mental energy spent
- Acceptance of what is

**Body appreciation:**
- Gratitude for function
- Respect for capabilities
- Care and maintenance
- Less focus on appearance

**Body love:**
- Genuine affection
- Comfort and ease
- Minimal appearance focus
- Full acceptance

Most healing moves people toward neutrality or appreciation—not necessarily love.

### Why Neutrality Matters

You don't have to love your body to have peace:
- Love can feel forced or fake
- Neutrality is sustainable
- Respect doesn't require positive feelings
- Function matters more than form

"I don't have to love my body. I just need to not hate it."

## Common Patterns

### Body Checking

Repetitive behaviors to monitor body:
- Mirror checking
- Weighing frequently
- Measuring or pinching
- Comparing to others
- Seeking reassurance

Body checking provides temporary relief but increases long-term distress.

### Body Avoidance

Avoiding confrontation with body:
- Avoiding mirrors
- Wearing concealing clothes
- Avoiding photos
- Skipping activities
- Not looking at body

Avoidance reduces short-term distress but maintains fear.

### Mental Rituals

Internal monitoring:
- Constant body awareness
- Comparing to others
- Planning what to eat/how to exercise
- Replaying body comments
- Imagining how you look to others

The mind stays occupied with body whether you want it to or not.

## The Healing Path

### What Healing Looks Like

Body image healing isn't about:
- Achieving the "right" body
- Learning to love every inch
- Never having negative thoughts
- Complete indifference

It is about:
- Reducing preoccupation
- Decreasing distress
- Expanding life beyond appearance
- Treating the body with respect
- Finding peace enough to live fully

### What This Course Covers

We'll explore:
- Where your body image came from
- How it affects your behavior
- How to challenge body beliefs
- Building body respect
- Living beyond appearance

This is gradual work. Be patient with yourself.

---

## Key Concepts

- **Body image:** How you perceive, think, feel, and behave about your body
- **Four dimensions:** Perceptual, affective, cognitive, behavioral
- **Not fixed:** Body image can change with awareness and intention
- **Spectrum:** From hatred to neutrality to appreciation—any movement helps
- **Common patterns:** Checking, avoidance, mental rituals
- **Healing goal:** Peace and reduced preoccupation, not necessarily love
