## Preparing the Body

Physical preparation often gets overlooked in favor of psychological preparation. But the body is where the experience happens.

**A well-prepared body supports smoother navigation and cleaner processing.**

---

## Why Physical Preparation Matters

### The Body Holds:
- Tension that blocks flow
- Emotions stored somatically
- Material that will surface
- The capacity to feel and process

### Physical Discomfort:
- Can distract from the work
- Can become amplified
- Can signal something needing attention
- Is easier when the body is prepared

### Cleaner Experience:
- Well-rested body processes better
- Clean diet reduces noise
- Physical health supports integration

---

## Timeline: Weeks Before

### Start Cleaning Up:
- Reduce or eliminate alcohol
- Reduce or eliminate cannabis
- Reduce processed foods
- Increase whole foods, vegetables
- Hydrate well

### Begin Body Practices:
- Movement: yoga, walking, exercise
- Stretching and releasing tension
- Breath work (gently)
- Adequate sleep

### Why the Lead Time:
- Physical changes take time
- You want a baseline of health
- Gradual transition is easier
- You're showing commitment to the process

## Diet Guidelines

### General Principles:
- Light, clean eating
- Reduce stimulants
- Reduce sugar
- Reduce heavy/hard-to-digest foods
- Increase hydration

### Substance-Specific:

**Ayahuasca:**
- Strict diet (dieta) often required
- No tyramine-rich foods (aged cheese, fermented foods)
- No pork, red meat often restricted
- Sexual abstinence often recommended
- Follow facilitator guidelines

**Psilocybin/LSD:**
- Lighter restrictions
- Clean eating recommended
- Some eat very light or fast day-of
- Avoid heavy meals close to experience

**MDMA:**
- Hydration important
- Avoid alcohol
- Some avoid caffeine

### Day Of:
- Light or no eating for several hours before
- Stay hydrated
- Avoid stimulants
- Follow specific guidelines for the substance

## Medications and Contraindications

### Critical to Research:
Different psychedelics interact differently with medications

### Common Interactions:
- **SSRIs** can diminish effects or cause serotonin syndrome (especially with MDMA)
- **MAOIs** are dangerous with many substances
- **Lithium** contraindicated with many psychedelics
- **Blood pressure medications** may interact

### What to Do:
- Research your specific medications
- Consult knowledgeable practitioners
- Don't stop medications abruptly
- Be completely honest with any guides/facilitators

### Withdrawal Considerations:
If tapering off medications, do so:
- Under medical supervision
- With adequate time before experience
- Not rushed for a ceremony

## Sleep and Rest

### Before the Experience:
- Be well-rested going in
- Prioritize sleep for several days before
- Don't arrive exhausted
- Your nervous system will be working hard

### Common Mistake:
Traveling far, arriving exhausted, doing ceremony immediately. This compromises the experience.

### Better:
Arrive early. Rest. Acclimate. Begin from a place of restoration.

---

## Physical Health Issues

### If You Have Conditions:
- Heart issues: consult medical professionals
- Breathing issues: may affect some experiences
- Chronic pain: may be amplified
- Physical disabilities: discuss accommodations

### Be Honest:
- With yourself about limitations
- With facilitators about conditions
- Don't hide relevant information
- Your safety depends on disclosure

---

## The Setting

### Physical Environment Matters:

**Safety:**
- Space where you won't be disturbed
- No driving required
- Hazards removed
- Someone available if needed

**Comfort:**
- Comfortable place to sit/lie
- Temperature controlled
- Bathroom accessible
- Blankets, pillows available

**Aesthetics:**
- Clean, uncluttered
- Pleasant to the senses
- Natural elements if possible
- Meaningful objects if desired

## Support People

### Consider Having:
- A sitter (someone sober to support)
- A facilitator (if available and appropriate)
- Someone to check on you afterward
- Emergency contact who knows what you're doing

### Qualities of Good Support:
- Calm, grounded presence
- Experience with psychedelics (ideally)
- Not intrusive
- Trustworthy
- Can handle intensity

### Solo Experiences:
- Higher risk
- Not recommended for beginners
- If chosen, extra preparation needed
- Have phone accessible, someone reachable

---

## Practical Logistics

### Before the Experience:
- Clear your schedule (day of and day after)
- Turn off phone or set to emergency-only
- Handle practical responsibilities
- Tell anyone who needs to know you're unavailable

### Have Available:
- Water
- Light snacks for after
- Journal and pen
- Comfortable clothing
- Music (if using)
- Eye mask (optional)
- Tissues
- Bucket (for possible nausea)

### Day After:
- Keep schedule clear
- Arrangements for rest
- No major responsibilities
- Integration time protected

## The Week Before

### Final Preparations:
- Finalize all logistics
- Continue clean eating
- Prioritize sleep
- Reduce stimulation
- Begin turning inward
- Notice what's arising

### Avoid:
- Major stress if possible
- Intense arguments
- Overpacking schedule
- Exhausting yourself

---

## Key Concepts

- **Body Matters:** Physical preparation supports the experience.

- **Diet:** Clean eating, substance-specific guidelines, light/fasting day-of.

- **Medications:** Research interactions, disclose to facilitators.

- **Setting:** Safe, comfortable, undisturbed space.

- **Logistics:** Clear schedule, support people, practical supplies.
