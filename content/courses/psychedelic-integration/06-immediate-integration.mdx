## The Critical Window

The experience isn't over when the effects wear off.

The first 24-48 hours after a psychedelic experience are crucial. This is when insights are fresh, neural pathways are plastic, and the work of integration is most potent.

**What you do now determines whether the experience transforms you or fades.**

---

## The Mistake Most People Make

### Common Pattern:
- Intense, meaningful experience
- Return to normal life immediately
- Check phone, talk to everyone
- Jump back into routine
- Insights fade within weeks
- Nothing really changes

### The Result:
The experience becomes a memory, not a transformation.

---

## Protecting the Space

### The First 24-48 Hours:

**Minimize:**
- Screens (especially social media)
- Stimulation
- Talking about the experience too much
- Normal routine
- Decisions
- Other people's energy

**Maximize:**
- Silence and stillness
- Time in nature
- Rest and sleep
- Gentle movement
- Being with the experience

## Journaling

Writing is the most important integration tool:

### When to Write:
- As soon as you're able after the experience
- While memories are fresh
- Before talking to anyone
- Daily in the following days

### What to Write:
- What happened (narrative)
- What you felt
- What you saw, heard, sensed
- Insights that arose
- Questions that remain
- What feels changed

### How to Write:
- Stream of consciousness
- Don't edit or analyze yet
- Include fragments and images
- Write more than you think necessary

## Being with What Arose

### Don't Rush to Understand:
- Analysis comes later
- For now, feel
- Let the experience integrate somatically
- Meaning emerges over time

### Stay with the Material:
- If grief arose, let yourself grieve
- If love arose, stay with the love
- If insight arose, sit with it
- Don't move on too quickly

### The Process Continues:
Integration isn't a single event. Material continues processing for days, weeks, sometimes longer.

---

## Common Post-Experience States

### You Might Feel:
- **Blissful/Open:** Afterglow, expanded heart
- **Raw/Vulnerable:** Exposed, sensitive
- **Tired:** Deep exhaustion
- **Confused:** Not sure what happened
- **Grateful:** Overwhelmed with appreciation
- **Sad:** Grief continuing to move
- **Anxious:** Disturbed by what arose

### All Are Normal:
There's no "right" way to feel. Honor whatever is present.

## Talking About It

### Be Selective:
- Not everyone will understand
- Not everyone needs to know
- Talking too much can dilute the experience
- Choose carefully who you share with

### Best to Talk To:
- Integration circle (if you have one)
- Therapist familiar with psychedelics
- Close friend who has experience
- The person who sat with you

### Avoid:
- Social media posting
- Telling everyone
- People who will be skeptical or dismissive
- Trying to convert others

### When You Do Talk:
- Speak from your experience, not universal truth
- Don't preach
- Stay humble
- Keep the most sacred parts private if needed

---

## Physical Care

### The Body Needs:
- Rest and sleep
- Hydration
- Nourishing food (when hunger returns)
- Gentle movement
- Nature if possible

### Avoid:
- Alcohol (especially)
- Substances
- Intense exercise
- Sexual activity (often recommended to wait)
- Physical strain

### The Body Is Processing Too:
Somatic integration is happening. Support it with care.

---

## What If It Was Difficult?

### After a Hard Experience:
- Extra gentleness with yourself
- May need more rest
- May need support/processing
- Don't pretend you're fine

### Normal Post-Difficulty Feelings:
- Shakiness
- Sensitivity
- Processing continuing
- Slow return to equilibrium

### When to Get Help:
- If disturbing content persists
- If you can't function
- If you're destabilized
- If old wounds are now raw

---

## The Days Following

### Days 2-7:
- Continue protecting space
- Keep journaling
- Dreams may be active
- Insights continue arriving
- Gentle re-entry to life

### Watch For:
- The pull to "get back to normal"
- Losing the thread
- Insights starting to fade
- Old patterns reasserting

### Maintain:
- Practices that support integration
- Connection to what you learned
- The new perspective

## Beginning to Understand

### After the Initial Period:
- You can begin to analyze
- What does it mean?
- What am I being asked to do?
- What needs to change?

### Questions for Reflection:
- What were the main themes?
- What message came through clearest?
- What am I being called to?
- What wants to change?
- What practices or actions emerge from this?

### But Don't Rush:
Full understanding often takes time. Some experiences reveal their meaning gradually.

---

## Key Concepts

- **Critical Window:** First 24-48 hours shape whether transformation sticks.

- **Protect the Space:** Minimize stimulation, maximize stillness.

- **Journal:** Most important integration tool—write while fresh.

- **Be Selective:** Choose carefully who you share with.

- **Don't Rush:** Understanding emerges over time.
