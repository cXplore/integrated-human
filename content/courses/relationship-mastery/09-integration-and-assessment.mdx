## Component 4: Knowledge Assessment (20%)

Complete the course quiz. You'll need 75% (9 of 12 correct) to pass.

The quiz is in the course assessment system. Review key concepts before taking it:

### Key Concepts Review:

**Attachment**
- Attachment styles are adaptive strategies, not fixed traits
- "Earned secure" attachment is possible through conscious work
- Pattern recognition is the foundation for change

**Nervous System**
- The window of tolerance is where regulation happens
- Flooding (100+ bpm) makes constructive conversation impossible
- Regulation must happen BEFORE communication

**Communication**
- Soft start-up predicts conflict outcome (96% accuracy)
- "I" statements express experience without attack
- Vulnerability requires safety, not just courage

**Conflict**
- 69% of conflicts are perpetual — the goal is dialogue, not solution
- The Four Horsemen (criticism, contempt, defensiveness, stonewalling) predict relationship failure
- Repair attempts are the key to successful conflict

**Repair**
- Repair predicts relationship success more than compatibility
- Understand impact BEFORE explaining intent
- Unrepaired ruptures accumulate into distance

**Boundaries**
- Boundaries are what YOU will do, not rules for others
- Boundaries without consequences are preferences
- Boundaries are required for healthy love

## After This Course

### The Work Continues

This course gave you frameworks, practice, and awareness. The work of relationships is lifelong.

What to continue:
- Daily nervous system regulation
- Pattern recognition as ongoing practice
- Regular repair when ruptures occur
- Ongoing vulnerable communication
- Boundary maintenance

### When You Need More

Some things require more than a course:
- If you're dealing with trauma, work with a trauma-informed therapist
- If your relationship has major issues, consider couples therapy
- If you're consistently stuck, individual therapy can help

### Returning to the Material

You can return to any module when needed. Module 3 (regulation) and Module 7 (repair) are worth revisiting regularly.
