## Before We Begin

This course is different from most relationship content you've encountered.

Most relationship advice gives you information. Tips. Frameworks. Things to think about. You read it, nod along, maybe feel inspired for a day, and nothing changes.

This course requires you to do things. Uncomfortable things. Things you can't complete by reading faster.

You will be asked to:
- Have conversations you've been avoiding
- Notice patterns you'd rather not see
- Practice skills that feel awkward at first
- Document what actually happens, not what you wish happened
- Spend weeks on material that could be "understood" in hours

The certificate at the end of this course means something. It means you did the work, over at least 8 weeks, and demonstrated actual skill development. Not that you watched videos. Not that you passed a quiz on concepts.

If that's not what you're looking for, this isn't the right course. There are plenty of places to get relationship information without the practice requirement.

If you're ready for actual change, keep going.

## Why Most Relationship Advice Fails

You've probably read about the Four Horsemen. You know what "I statements" are. You've heard that you should "communicate better" and "set boundaries."

And yet.

Your patterns persist. Your fights still escalate the same way. Your triggers still hijack you. The gap between what you know and what you do remains vast.

Here's why:

### 1. Information Doesn't Change Behavior

Knowing that contempt destroys relationships doesn't stop contempt from arising in your body when your partner does that thing they do.

Knowing you "should" take a break when flooded doesn't mean you can actually do it in the moment.

Knowing your attachment style doesn't rewire your nervous system's responses.

Information operates in the neocortex. Your relational patterns operate in the limbic system and brainstem. They're triggered faster than thought. By the time you "know" what you should do, you've already reacted.

**Change requires practice, not just understanding.** Repeated experience that builds new neural pathways. Actual situations where you try something different and see what happens.

### 2. Concepts Without Precision

Most relationship content speaks in generalities.

"Be more vulnerable."
"Communicate your needs."
"Work on your attachment issues."

These are about as useful as telling someone to "be healthier." Technically true. Practically useless.

What specific patterns do YOU run? Not "anxious attachment" in general — what does YOUR anxiety look like? What triggers it? How does it show up in your body? What do you do when it's activated?

Until you can answer these questions with precision about YOUR patterns, you're working with abstractions instead of reality.

### 3. Individual Focus on Relational Problems

You can't fix a relationship by working only on yourself.

Well, you can work on yourself — your triggers, your patterns, your regulation. That matters.

But relationships are systems. What you do affects what they do affects what you do. The patterns are co-created. The dance happens between you.

This course will ask you to work on yourself AND to practice with others. To have actual conversations. To try things in real relationships and see what happens.

### 4. No Feedback Loop

How do you know if you're getting better at relationships?

If you're learning to play piano, you can hear whether the notes are right. If you're learning to code, the program either works or it doesn't.

But relationship skills? People rarely get accurate feedback. Your partner might not tell you what's landing and what isn't. You might not even notice your own patterns.

This course builds in feedback loops. You'll document what happens. You'll analyze real conflicts. You'll track patterns over time. You'll have evidence of whether things are shifting.

## What This Course Actually Is

This is a practicum, not a lecture series.

### The Structure:

Each module has two components:

**Understanding (in-course)**
- Deep exploration of a relationship domain
- Not surface-level tips — the actual dynamics
- Precise enough to apply, not vague inspiration

**Practice (real life)**
- Structured exercises to do between modules
- Real conversations, real conflicts, real relationships
- Documentation of what happened and what you learned

You cannot meaningfully complete this course in a weekend. The practice periods require actual time in actual relationships.

### What You'll Document:

Throughout this course, you'll build a Practice Log:
- Exercises you completed
- What happened
- What you noticed
- What you learned
- Evidence of skill development

This isn't busywork. This is how change happens. And this documentation is part of earning the certificate.

### The Assessment:

At the end of the course, you'll complete a comprehensive assessment:
- A written analysis of your relational patterns and how they've shifted
- Your complete practice log
- Demonstration of key skills
- Knowledge check

This is how the certificate means something.

## The Core Skills

This course develops specific, practical skills:

### 1. Pattern Recognition
The ability to see your relational patterns in real-time — not after the fact, but as they're happening. "Oh, this is me doing that thing."

### 2. Regulation Under Fire
The ability to regulate your nervous system when activated in relationship — not theoretically, but actually, when it matters.

### 3. Vulnerable Communication
The ability to speak what's true for you, directly, without violence or withdrawal — especially the hard things.

### 4. Conflict Navigation
The ability to disagree, even fight, without the Four Horsemen — and to recognize and interrupt them when they appear.

### 5. Repair
The ability to return from rupture — to acknowledge impact, take responsibility, and genuinely reconnect.

### 6. Boundary Holding
The ability to set and maintain limits that protect you without closing your heart.

Each skill is developed through understanding AND practice. Neither alone is sufficient.

## Your First Practice: The Observation Week

Before diving into content about what you "should" do, you need clear data on what you actually do.

This week's practice is observation only. Don't try to change anything. Just notice.

<Exercise title="Week 1 Practice: The Observation Log">

### Instructions:

For the next 7 days, keep a simple log. Each day, note:

1. **Activation moments:** When did you get triggered in a relationship context? (Any relationship — romantic, family, friends, work.) What happened? What did you feel in your body?

2. **Pattern sightings:** What familiar patterns showed up? Things you've done before. Your "go-to" responses.

3. **What you avoided:** What conversations didn't you have? What did you not say? What did you pretend was fine when it wasn't?

### Log Template (Copy and use daily):

## A Note on Honesty

This course only works if you're honest — with yourself, in your exercises, in your documentation.

No one is grading your life. No one will judge you for having messy patterns. Everyone has messy patterns.

The practice log isn't for show. It's for you to see your reality clearly enough to change it.

If you find yourself sanitizing your responses, notice that. That's probably a pattern too.
