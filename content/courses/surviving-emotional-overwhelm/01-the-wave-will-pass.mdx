## When Emotions Feel Unsurvivable

Sometimes emotions get so intense they feel like they'll destroy you. The grief, the rage, the despair, the panic—it's too much. You can't breathe. You can't think. You can't do anything but feel.

In these moments, one truth can save you:

**This will pass.**

Not because you'll fix it. Not because you'll figure it out. But because intense emotions have a natural life cycle. They rise, they peak, and they fall. Every single time.

---

## The 90-Second Rule

Neuroanatomist Jill Bolte Taylor discovered something important:

> The physiological lifespan of an emotion in the body is about 90 seconds.

What does this mean? The chemical surge of any emotion—the actual physical cascade—lasts about a minute and a half.

When emotions last longer, it's because we're:
- Retriggering them with thoughts
- Fighting them (which intensifies them)
- Feeding them with rumination

The raw emotion itself is brief. It's what we do with it that extends the suffering.

---

## The Wave Metaphor

Think of intense emotion like a wave:

1. **The swell** — You feel it building
2. **The rise** — Intensity increases rapidly
3. **The crest** — The peak, the most intense moment
4. **The fall** — Intensity begins to decrease
5. **The settling** — The wave passes, leaving you on shore

You cannot stop the wave. But you can learn to ride it instead of drowning in it.

---

<Callout type="important">
No emotion, no matter how intense, stays at peak intensity forever. The wave will crest. And then it will fall. Your only job is to survive until it does.
</Callout>

---

## What Makes Emotions Worse

When a wave of emotion hits, we instinctively try to:

**Fight it**
- "I shouldn't feel this way"
- "This is wrong"
- "I need to stop this"

Fighting adds resistance. Resistance creates more turbulence.

**Feed it**
- Rehearsing what caused it
- Building the case for why it's justified
- Imagining future scenarios

Feeding keeps retriggering the wave, so it never settles.

**Flee from it**
- Numbing (substances, distraction)
- Dissociating
- Suppressing

Fleeing doesn't stop the wave—it just delays it or pushes it underground.

---

## The Alternative: Riding

Instead of fight, feed, or flee, we can ride:

- Accept that the wave is here
- Let it move through without resistance
- Trust that it will pass
- Keep yourself safe while it does

Riding isn't passive. It takes tremendous courage to stay present with intense emotion. But it's the fastest way through.
