The blue light from screens suppresses melatonin and keeps your brain in alert mode. A digital sunset creates space for your nervous system to prepare for sleep.

## How to Practice

### Set Your Sunset Time

Choose a time 60-90 minutes before your target bedtime. At this time, screens stop.

**What counts as screens:**
- Phone
- Computer
- Tablet
- TV
- E-readers with backlight

### Create the Boundary

1. Set an alarm or reminder for your digital sunset
2. When it goes off, put devices in a designated spot
3. Switch to screenless activities

### What to Do Instead

**Relaxing activities:**
- Reading (paper books or e-ink devices)
- Journaling
- Gentle stretching
- Conversation
- Puzzles or low-key games
- Taking a bath
- Preparing for tomorrow
- Listening to calm music or audiobooks

### Make It Automatic

- Charge devices outside the bedroom
- Use phone's bedtime mode for automatic dimming
- Have a book visible and ready
- Create a consistent wind-down routine

## Why It Works

Screens stimulate the brain and suppress melatonin, making sleep onset harder and sleep quality worse. The digital sunset lets your circadian system do its jobâ€”preparing your body for restorative sleep.

## Common Challenges

**FOMO**: Nothing happens after your sunset that can't wait until morning.

**"I need my phone for alarm"**: Buy a $10 alarm clock.

**"I use it for audiobooks"**: Use a dedicated player, or put phone in airplane mode across the room.
